Executive Summary

In-Context World Modeling for Robotic Control scores 52/100 for a mix of novelty, practical impact, and relevance to AI-agent and software-engineering workflows. Modern Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models often fail to generalize to novel setups, such as altered camera viewpoints or robot morphologies, because they are typically conditioned only on current observations and language instructions. By ignoring the underlying system configuration as a variable, these models implicitly assume a fixed execution context encountered during training, necessitating data-intensive fine-tuning for any new environment.
